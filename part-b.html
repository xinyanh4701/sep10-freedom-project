My Visionary Ideas to Improve Mass Public Transportation

Tech Invention Idea #1

  • Wearable on your hand
  • Looks like a simple, sleek, modern watch
  • Tap the side of the watch for a holographic app to appear
  • The hologram will display in a 16:10 aspect ratio
  • The hologram includes the following information:
    • What transportation vehicles are arriving or departing
    • What time is it?
    • What public transportation stations are available
    • What public transportation services are available
    • When is the next stop or what stop is that called
    • How much time do you have to wait for the vehicle to arrive
    • Personal information
  • Wake you up with vibrations and sound to help you get to your destination if you are asleep
    • The watch will use sensors to detect the heart rate of your body to see if you are sleeping

Tech Invention Idea #2

  • Located in poles or somewhere high up that can detect a big area
  • Uses cameras, lidar, and motion sensors to detect how many people are waiting for the vehicle to arrive
  • Help reduce passenger overboard
  • If the device shows that there are too many people are waiting for the vehicle to arrive, there are two options to solve the issue(all of them run on the same route):
    • Have standby vehicles that are ready to carry some of the passengers
    • The vehicle that is closest to the destination would make an express
      • For example, if a train is 5 minutes away from the very crowded destination and there are not a lot of passengers between the crowded destination and the stop the vehicle is at, then, the train would tell the passengers to leave if they are not going to the crowded destination and would go straight to the crowded destination. The train behind the express train would have to pick up the pace.
  • Collects data of how many people use the transportation often
    • If there are a lot of people using the vehicle at a certain time, then the schedules would change according to the data.

Tech Invention Idea #3

  • Located outside of the vehicle (next to the doors)
  • Detects if people are going get in or out of the vehicle
  • Lets the driver know if people are still getting in or out of the vehicle
  • Reduces injuries or property damage
    • When elderly people get off the vehicle, the driver or operator might close the doors when the person is still getting out. That might cause injuries to the elderly people since the elderly population is more prone to injuries. This applies to inanimate objects.

Outro

  • You would never know what could happen over the decade in the public transportation industry. The future of this industry is worth the wait.
